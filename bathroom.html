Primary Bathroom

Three Options · Cosmetic Refresh to Full Cabinet Replacement · $300–$4,400

The decision structure: Option A (tile only) can stand alone. Options A+B (tile + full replacement) should be done together if chosen. Option C (reface) is an alternative to doing A+B — similar scope and cost, different approach. You pick one path: A alone, A+B together, or C.

What Drives the Decision

Condition of existing cabinet

Open the vanity doors and inspect the box construction. If the case is solid (no swelling, no water damage at base), Option C (reface) is viable and saves demo cost. If the box is damaged or very old, full replacement (A+B) makes more sense — you won't want to put new fronts on a deteriorating box.

Timing with bedroom renovation

Do the bathroom during bedroom construction. Your contractor will already be mobilized for the bedroom ceiling/flooring work. Adding bathroom cabinet work to that visit costs far less than a separate mobilization. This is the primary reason to do A+B (or C) rather than defer — the savings from shared mobilization are real.

Budget priority

If the bathroom budget is tight, Option A alone ($300-$800) is a legitimate standalone improvement. New tile behind the sink makes a visible difference without touching the cabinet or plumbing. It can be done later, independently, by a tile contractor in a day.

What Is Not in Scope

None of these options include: new fixtures (faucet, shower hardware, towel bars), shower tile, toilet replacement, or plumbing relocation. Those are separate projects. The plumbing cluster (cast iron, hose bibs) has already been completed — no plumbing work is required for the bathroom cabinet options above.

Sequence

  • Decide option before bedroom construction starts
  • If doing A+B or C: coordinate with general contractor handling bedroom
  • Tile can be scheduled after cabinet work is complete (1-day job)
  • If doing A only: can be deferred to any point, independent of bedroom timeline
