Great Room — Paul McCobb Planner Group Option

Whole-House Design System · Maple Case Goods + Walnut Upholstery · 60-75% Savings

The Smart Strategy: Use Paul McCobb Planner Group case goods (tables, consoles, credenzas) in both great room AND bedroom, combined with modern upholstered pieces (walnut legs). Creates unified design vocabulary throughout the house while saving $20,000-25,000.

Why Planner Group for Great Room

Paul McCobb designed Planner Group specifically to work in ANY room of the house. The key quote: "The entire line could be used in any room of the house — pieces and combinations fit as well in the living room as they did in the bedroom, or dining room."

This modular flexibility made it the best-selling furniture line of the 1950s. It's perfect for your 1960 house.

Great Room Planner Group Strategy

The Mix Formula (Not Boring!):

  • Planner Group maple case goods = coffee table, end tables, console/credenza (clean structure)
  • Modern upholstered pieces with walnut legs = Jasper sofa, Quinn chairs, Callan chair, Afton chairs (warmth + comfort)
  • Your existing character pieces = Piano, rugs, textiles, triangle table, buffet (life + history)

Result: Maple + walnut + upholstery + textiles = layered, sophisticated, NOT monotonous

Design Cohesion: Maple + Walnut Strategy

Two wood tones throughout the house = sophisticated mid-century design:

  • Maple Planner Group (case goods) - lighter, clean, structural
  • Walnut legs (upholstered pieces) - warm, grounding
  • Result: Visual interest + layering vs. monotone everything-matches

The Quinn chair is the bridge: Appears in both great room and bedroom with walnut legs, connecting the spaces while Planner Group maple provides unified case good vocabulary.

Your existing pieces add life: Piano, Persian rugs, wall textiles, green/brown carpets, triangle table, buffet, wrought iron chandelier and stools = warmth, history, personality

Would This Be Boring? No.

Boring would be: Every piece Planner Group maple, no contrast, no textiles, showroom-like

This approach is layered:

  • Maple structure (clean, refined)
  • Walnut warmth (grounding, rich)
  • Upholstery variety (Sunbrella sofa, performance velvet Quinn, leather Afton/Boden/Callan)
  • Your textiles (wall hangings, rugs adding color/pattern)
  • Vintage character pieces (piano, Chinese chest, triangle table)
  • Black metal accents (wrought iron)

Result: Cohesive but not matchy-matchy. Unified but interesting. Clean but warm.
