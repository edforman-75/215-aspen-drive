Vintage 1960s Furniture Option

Authentic Period-Correct Pieces · Solid Walnut · 50-67% Cost Savings

Most Authentic Choice: Vintage mid-century modern furniture from 1960-1965 is period-correct for your 1960 house. Real solid walnut (not stained rubberwood), superior joinery from that era, and true to the architectural intent of the home.

Budget: Complete vintage sets range from $2,500-5,500 vs Hudson $11,203 — saving $5,700-8,700 (50-67% less)

7. Glenn of California

Period: 1950s-1960s | Origin: Arcadia, California

Why it's relevant: A California manufacturer — pure Western MCM pedigree. Glenn collaborated with designers including Milo Baughman (before his work with Thayer Coggin), producing walnut furniture with a ranch-house sensibility that aligns closely with Sun Valley architecture. Harder to find complete bedroom sets but very period-appropriate.

Pieces: $300-1,200 each | Sets rare, price varies

What to look for:

  • Glenn of California walnut credenzas and case pieces
  • Low dressers with tapered legs and flush hardware
  • Often labeled on back panel or inside drawer

Note: Glenn pieces show up more often at California estate sales and West Coast dealers. 1stDibs and Chairish are the best bets nationally.

8. Broyhill Sculptra

9. Founders Furniture

  • Real solid walnut (matches great room)

What "King Compatible" Means in Vintage

Most 1960s bedroom suites were sized for full (double) or queen. King beds were uncommon. A "complete vintage set" therefore typically means a headboard only — the dresser, chest, and nightstands work regardless of bed size.

Strategy: Don't filter for "king bed" — it will exclude most sets. Instead, source the case goods (dresser, nightstands, chest) as a set, then find a compatible king headboard separately — or use the steel frame insert strategy (see Vintage + Steel option) to pair any vintage headboard with a modern king steel frame.

Fallback if no king headboard is available: Two matching twin headboards mounted side-by-side on a king steel frame. This is the default plan under the Vintage + Steel option and works well — the visual result is nearly identical to a single king headboard, and twin headboards are far more plentiful in the vintage market.

Inspection Checklist

When evaluating vintage pieces:

  • Solid wood (not veneer) — check sides and back
  • Dovetail joinery in drawers (sign of quality)
  • Original hardware present
  • Drawer slides functional (may need updating)
  • Minimal water damage or veneer lifting
  • Matching grain/color across pieces
  • Structural integrity (no wobbling)
  • Refinishing needed? (budget $500-1,500)
