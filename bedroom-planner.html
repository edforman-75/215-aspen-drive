About Paul McCobb Planner Group

The Planner Group was first released in 1949 and remained in continuous production until 1964. It became one of the most successful furniture lines of the mid-20th century because of its:

  • Modular versatility - Pieces work in living room, bedroom, or dining room
  • Clean design - Minimal ornamentation, honest materials, functional beauty
  • Solid construction - Made from solid maple (occasionally walnut or birch)
  • Affordable modernism - Quality design at accessible prices
  • Mix-and-match system - Chests, cabinets on legs or platform bases

Maple vs Walnut - Intentional Design Choice

Using maple Planner Group in bedroom with walnut great room is good design:

  • Bedroom: All maple Planner Group = calm, restful, cohesive sleeping space
  • Great room: If using Planner Group maple case goods + walnut-leg upholstery = visual interest, two wood tones
  • Bridge: Quinn chair appears in both rooms (walnut legs tie to great room)
  • Result: Connected but not identical - intentional contrast

Two wood tones (maple + walnut) throughout a house is a classic mid-century design strategy. Creates layering and sophistication vs. monotone everything-matches approach.
