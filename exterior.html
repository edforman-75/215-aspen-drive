Exterior Renovation — Good / Better / Best

Mountain Modern Restoration · Three Tiers

The House

1960 two-story residence in Warm Springs, Sun Valley. Currently 4 BR / 3 BA vacation rental facing Baldy and Warm Springs ski runs through mature aspens. Low-pitched roof with deep overhangs, exposed beam ends (rafter tails), strong horizontal emphasis. Decades of incremental updates have obscured the original midcentury modern design.

What We're Working With

  • Upper level: Rough-textured stucco, painted cream/tan
  • Lower level: Split-face concrete block, painted cream/yellow
  • Windows (6 upstairs): Original aluminum, not thermally broken
  • Sliding doors (4 sets): 72×80" with beige exterior cladding
  • Deck railings: Vertical 2×2 wood balusters, 4×4 posts
  • Deck/beams: Wood plank, exposed rafter tails at eave line
  • Entry screens: Existing Larson storm doors
  • Roofline: Low-pitched with deep overhangs (NOT being modified — the star feature)

GOOD Tier — "Refreshed" ($7K–$12K)

What You Get: House looks intentional instead of neglected. Coordinated warm mauve palette ties stucco, block, railings, beams, deck. Beige cladding becomes a deliberate lighter accent.

What Changes Aesthetically: Goes from "random mountain rental" to "thoughtful warm palette." Dark-stained railings/beams/deck add depth against mauve body.

What Changes Functionally: Minimal. Fresh paint protects surfaces. No thermal improvement, no view improvement, rough texture persists.

Element Details:

  • Stucco: Repaint flat/matte. BM Smoked Oyster (2109-40) or Hint of Mauve (2097-50) or Sequoia (AF-270)
  • Block: Repaint same body color
  • Railings: Keep balusters, restain dark
  • Deck/beams: Light restain, focus on rafter tails
  • Front door: Restain dark walnut
  • Entry screens: Keep existing Larson
  • Windows/sliders: No change

BETTER Tier — "Designed" ($22K–$36K)

What You Gain Over Good: Three transformative upgrades:

  1. Dark-framed Milgard A250 sliders eliminate beige cladding — opens entire color palette
  2. Smooth stucco "Santa Barbara" finish — biggest visual impact per dollar
  3. Cable rail replaces balusters — opens Baldy/Warm Springs view

What Changes Functionally: Significant thermal upgrade at largest openings. Cable rail easier to maintain. Smooth stucco sheds water better.

Element Details:

  • Sliders (4): Milgard A250, 72×80", dark bronze/black. Dealer: Viewpoint, 1009 Warm Springs Rd, Ketchum, (208) 726-2213
  • Stucco: Skim coat "Santa Barbara smooth" or "California smooth"
  • Block: Dark masonry stain (charcoal/iron grey)
  • Railings: Feeney CableRail retrofit, keep existing posts
  • Entry screens (2): Casper DIY retractable. $329/door, self-install 30 min, black/brown frame, magnetic closure. casperscreens.com or Lowe's. Lifetime frame warranty, 3yr mesh.
  • Front door: New or restain dark walnut

BEST Tier — "Restored" ($32K–$55K)

What You Gain Over Better: Completes Mountain Modern restoration:

  1. Kolbe VistaLuxe AL upstairs windows — thinnest thermally broken sightlines (1"), dark frames, complete thermal envelope
  2. Strip block paint to natural concrete — reveals original 1960 material expression
  3. Custom steel cable rail intermediates — code-compliant with elegant detail
  4. Glass-panel entry doors — Simpson contemporary wood doors with integrated glass lites

What Changes Functionally: Complete thermal envelope. All 10 glazing units thermally broken. Natural concrete more durable than painted (no moisture trapping). Dramatically more comfortable in winter.

Element Details:

  • Windows (6): Kolbe VistaLuxe AL LINE. 3 casements (34×47"), 3 awnings (35×22"). 1" sightlines, 50+ colors. Dealer: Glass Masters, 118 Lewis St, Ketchum, (208) 726-1420
  • Sliders (4): Milgard A250 or Kolbe VistaLuxe AL
  • Stucco: Skim coat + neutral paint (warm grey/putty/greige)
  • Block: BioBlast professional stripping (Alan Barr, Wood River Valley) + clear masonry sealer
  • Railings: Feeney CableRail + custom 1/4"×1.5" flat steel bar intermediates, powder-coated dark
  • Entry doors (2): Simpson contemporary wood with glass panels, paired with Phantom retractable screens
  • Entry screens (2): Phantom retractable, $650–900 ea installed. phantomscreens.com
