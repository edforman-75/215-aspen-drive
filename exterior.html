Note: Stucco Repair Is Required Regardless of Tier

The structural work being done on the deck will disturb and damage the adjacent stucco. Patching and repainting that area is not optional — it must be done as part of the deck repair. This has two implications:

  • Stucco repair cost is essentially a sunk cost — it exists in every tier whether you plan for it or not
  • Since you are already patching and repainting stucco, the incremental cost of doing the entire stucco exterior at the same time is reduced — the mobilization, staging, and paint-matching work is already happening
  • Partial repair may look worse than no repair — a fresh patch against weathered original stucco will be visible. Doing the whole surface avoids this mismatch.

Practical takeaway: Budget for full stucco work at whatever tier you choose. The deck repair makes it necessary to engage a stucco contractor in any case — at that point, doing the full exterior is the logical step.

Bridge Option: Paint Existing Slider Cladding

Before replacing sliders ($10,000-$16,000), consider painting the existing beige/tan aluminum cladding dark bronze/black.

Cost: ~$400-$800 (professional aluminum paint + labor for 4 sliders)

Process:

  • Clean and scuff aluminum cladding (TSP wash + light sanding)
  • Prime with aluminum-specific bonding primer (DTM or Stix)
  • Two coats exterior acrylic in dark bronze/black
  • Expected lifespan: 3-5 years before repainting needed

Advantages:

  • Immediate visual improvement for 5% the cost of replacement
  • Opens up dark stucco/block color palette without beige conflict
  • Fully reversible — can still replace sliders later if paint doesn't hold up
  • Good thermal performance already (existing sliders likely thermally broken)

Considerations:

  • Paint adhesion on aluminum can be challenging (proper prep critical)
  • May need touch-up or repainting in 3-5 years
  • Won't improve thermal performance (existing sliders unchanged)
  • Test on one slider first before committing to all four

Strategy: Paint cladding now (~$600), live with it for 1-2 years. If paint fails or you want better thermal performance, upgrade to new Milgard A250 sliders later. The paint investment isn't wasted — it proves the concept and gets you dark frames immediately while deferring the major expense.

REQUIRED Before BEST Tier: Block Paint Strip Test Area

Do not proceed with full block paint stripping ($500-$1,500) without first testing a representative area. This step is mandatory.

Why this is essential: Sixty years of paint over split-face concrete block is unpredictable. The natural concrete may be:

  • Beautiful — varied grey tones, texture, honest material expression
  • Stained, patchy, or discolored from decades of paint absorption
  • Uneven — paint may have penetrated some areas more than others
  • Revealing old repairs, different block batches, or color variation

What to strip for the test:

  • Choose the most visible wall — the one you will look at every day
  • Minimum 4'×4' area, ideally a full wall section between corners
  • Include both block faces and mortar joints in the test area
  • Use BioBlast or equivalent (contact Alan Barr, Wood River Valley) for chemical strip

Cost of test area: ~$150-$300 (materials + 2-3 hours labor)

What to evaluate after stripping:

  • Color — is the natural concrete warm or cold in tone?
  • Uniformity — does it look intentional or splotchy?
  • Texture — does the split-face character read clearly?
  • Interaction with stucco — do the two materials read well together?
  • Live with it through different light conditions (morning, midday, dusk)

Outcomes:

  • If beautiful: Proceed with full BioBlast treatment on all painted block
  • If marginal: Apply clear masonry sealer to test area, evaluate if sealer improves appearance enough
  • If disappointing: Repaint test area to match, proceed with BETTER tier painted block instead

This test area is not optional. The stripping decision is irreversible once done at scale. A $200 test protects a decision worth $500-$1,500 in labor and the permanent character of the house.

How to Order Paint Samples

Peel-and-Stick Samples (Recommended)

Order 12"×12" peel-and-stick paint samples from Samplize.com — about $6 each. These are actual paint, not printed, so colors are accurate.

  1. Order all colors from your chosen tier (GOOD: 4 colors, BETTER/BEST: 5-6 colors)
  2. Test on BOTH brick AND stucco surfaces in different lighting conditions
  3. Hold samples next to the beige window cladding — this is the critical compatibility test
  4. View in snow light (winter), full sun (summer), and shade (year-round)
  5. Test at different times: morning sun, midday, dusk
